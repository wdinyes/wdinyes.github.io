An interesting [feature](https://www.schneier.com/blog/archives/2026/04/possible-us-government-iphone-hacking-tool-leaked.html) from Schneier On Security.  What appears to have happened is that the US government spent millions of dollars creating a hacking tool for iPhones (which, incidentally, completely fails if Lockdown Mode is enabled - be sure to stay up to date and use Lockdown Mode if you feel you are at risk, folks).  Said tool was then [sold to the Russians](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/24/former-l3harris-trenchant-boss-jailed-for-selling-hacking-tools-to-russian-broker/) by one of the employees of the contracting company that did the dirty work.

This is in no way surprising.

This is a concrete example of why politicians are idiots when they ask for backdoors in encryption and then pinky-promise that it will only be used for Good Purposes&trade;. I mean, I'm sure they thought the same thing when they approved whatever black bag of tax dollars was used to create these hacking tools.  And I'm sure that L3Harris went full on ["I'm super trustworthy"](https://www.penny-arcade.com/comic/2012/01/25/hope-springs-eternal) in order to get the contract.  Darth Trenchant is a name you can trust.

But here we are again.

The only thing that can stop a bad guy with a gun is a good guy with a gun.  Uh huh.  I guess the trick is finding the good guy. I mean, one who isn't for sale for a paltry $1.3M.
